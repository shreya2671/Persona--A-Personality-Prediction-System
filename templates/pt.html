Extraversion


Extraversion is characterized by energy, positive emotions, and the tendency to seek stimulation and the company of others. Extroverts are often enthusiastic, action-oriented, and enjoy being around people. They thrive in social situations and are often perceived as talkative and assertive.

Neuroticism


Neuroticism refers to the tendency to experience negative emotions, such as anger, anxiety, or depression. People high in neuroticism are more likely to be emotionally reactive and vulnerable to stress. They may perceive ordinary situations as threatening and experience minor frustrations.

Agreeableness


Agreeableness reflects individual differences in concern for cooperation and social harmony. Agreeable individuals value getting along with others and are generally considerate, kind, generous, trusting, and trustworthy. They are often helpful and willing to compromise their interests with others.

Conscientiousness


Conscientiousness is characterized by self-discipline, carefulness, thoroughness, and a desire to perform tasks well. Conscientious individuals are goal-oriented and driven to succeed. They are often organized, dependable, and hardworking, and they show a strong sense of duty and responsibility.

Openness


Openness to experience (or simply openness) describes a dimension of cognitive style that distinguishes imaginative, creative people from down-to-earth, conventional people. Open individuals are intellectually curious, open to emotion, sensitive to beauty, and willing to try new things.
